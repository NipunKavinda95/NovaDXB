NOVADXB — SEASONAL & EVENTS GUIDE FOR DUBAI
=============================================================

---------------------------------------------------------
MONTH BY MONTH GUIDE
---------------------------------------------------------

JANUARY
Weather: 14-24°C. Sunny and perfect. Lowest humidity of the year.
Crowds: High season — book in advance.
Events: Dubai Shopping Festival (late Dec to late Jan) — massive sales discounts and entertainment across all malls.
What to do: Everything outdoor. Desert safari. Beach. All attractions.
Best for: First-time visitors wanting the complete Dubai experience.
Packing: Light layers for evenings — can get cool after sunset.

FEBRUARY
Weather: 15-26°C. Excellent.
Crowds: High season. Valentine's week extremely busy.
Events: Dubai Shopping Festival continues. UAE Desert Classic golf tournament.
What to do: All outdoor activities perfect. Dubai Marathon event.
Best for: Couples — Valentine's week in Dubai is spectacular.
Packing: Light clothing. Cardigan for evenings.

MARCH
Weather: 18-30°C. Still excellent but warming up.
Crowds: High season winding down. Slightly less crowded than Jan-Feb.
Events: Art Dubai (world-class art fair at DIFC). Dubai Food Festival.
What to do: All outdoor activities. Beach ideal. Desert before heat builds.
Best for: Art lovers and food enthusiasts.
Packing: Light clothing. No jacket needed.

APRIL
Weather: 22-36°C. Getting warm. Last comfortable outdoor month.
Crowds: Medium — transitioning from high to shoulder season.
Events: Dubai Food Festival continues. Various cultural events.
What to do: Morning outdoor activities. Afternoons in malls and indoor venues.
Best for: Budget-conscious travellers — prices start dropping as peak ends.
Packing: Light summer clothing. Sunscreen essential.

MAY
Weather: 28-41°C. Hot. Humidity increasing.
Crowds: Low. Prices significantly lower.
Events: Smaller local events.
What to do: Indoor activities mornings and evenings. Beach early morning or after sunset. Excellent pool time at hotels.
Best for: Budget travellers and shoppers — mall experiences, indoor attractions.
Packing: Full summer. High SPF sunscreen. Hat and sunglasses essential.

JUNE
Weather: 31-42°C. Very hot and humid.
Crowds: Low. Great hotel deals.
Events: Dubai Summer Surprises begins — mall entertainment and shopping deals.
What to do: Dubai Aquarium, IMG Worlds, indoor malls, late evening dining.
Best for: Maximum value — hotel prices lowest of the year.
Packing: As light as possible. Air conditioning everywhere is very cold — bring a light layer for indoors.

JULY
Weather: 33-43°C. Peak heat. Very high humidity.
Crowds: Very low. Cheapest time to visit.
Events: Dubai Summer Surprises. Many residents leave on holiday.
What to do: Almost entirely indoor. Mall attractions, cinema, spa, waterparks (early morning). Excellent fine dining availability and value.
Best for: Spa and wellness retreats. Indoor experiences. Maximum luxury at lowest prices.
Packing: Summer. Bring layers for extreme air conditioning indoors.

AUGUST
Weather: 33-44°C. Hottest month. Extremely humid.
Crowds: Very low.
Events: Eid Al Adha celebrations (date varies annually) — spectacular if visiting during this time.
What to do: Indoor only during day. Beautiful warm evenings after 9pm for outdoor dining.
Best for: Ultra-budget luxury. 5-star hotels at 3-star prices.
Packing: Minimal summer clothing.

SEPTEMBER
Weather: 30-40°C. Still hot but humidity decreasing slightly.
Crowds: Low but increasing. Back to school effect.
Events: GITEX Technology Week (late October — world's largest tech event).
What to do: Transition month. Some outdoor activity possible in evenings.
Best for: Tech professionals. Good value still available.
Packing: Light summer.

OCTOBER
Weather: 25-37°C. Noticeable improvement. Evenings pleasant.
Crowds: Medium increasing. Prices starting to rise.
Events: GITEX Technology Week (massive — 200,000 visitors). Global Village opens (late October).
What to do: Outdoor activities returning. Desert viable in evenings. Beach returning.
Best for: Tech enthusiasts. Beginning of outdoor season.
Packing: Light summer to light casual.

NOVEMBER
Weather: 20-32°C. Very comfortable. Perfect.
Crowds: Growing. High season beginning.
Events: Global Village fully open. Dubai Airshow (alternate years). Miracle Garden opens.
What to do: Everything. Perfect balance of weather activities and value before peak December prices.
Best for: The best overall value-to-experience month in the whole year.
Packing: Light casual. Light layer for evenings.

DECEMBER
Weather: 16-28°C. Perfect. Minimal humidity.
Crowds: Very high. Most expensive month. Book everything 2-3 months ahead.
Events: UAE National Day December 2-3 (spectacular — fireworks and celebrations). New Year's Eve (world-famous Dubai fireworks at Burj Khalifa — plan months ahead).
What to do: Everything. The whole city is beautifully decorated and energised.
Best for: Special occasions. New Year. The ultimate Dubai experience.
Packing: Light layers. Evening jacket or smart layer recommended.

---------------------------------------------------------
RAMADAN GUIDE (dates shift annually — check Islamic calendar)
---------------------------------------------------------
What changes during Ramadan:
- Eating drinking and smoking in public strictly prohibited from dawn to sunset.
- Many restaurants close during the day or open only for delivery.
- After sunset (Iftar) the city comes alive — best street food atmosphere of the year.
- Nightlife significantly reduced. Alcohol service restricted.
- Working hours reduced for Muslim employees.
- Dress code more strictly observed — visitors should cover up more than usual.

What is better during Ramadan:
- Iftar buffets at hotels (AED 150-300 per person) — extraordinary food and atmosphere.
- Ramadan tents with live music and cultural performances.
- Late night Suhoor dining after midnight — magical atmosphere.
- Crowds lighter at major attractions during the day.
- The spiritual and cultural atmosphere is genuinely unique and beautiful.

Visitor advice for Ramadan:
- Be respectful and do not eat drink or smoke in public areas.
- Experience at least one Iftar meal — it is a highlight of any Dubai visit during this period.
- The city is not closed — most tourist attractions remain open.
- Check specific venue hours as they shift significantly during Ramadan.

---------------------------------------------------------
KEY ANNUAL EVENTS CALENDAR
---------------------------------------------------------
Dubai Shopping Festival: Late December to late January — city-wide sales and entertainment.
Dubai Marathon: January — one of the world's great city marathons.
Art Dubai: March — Middle East's leading international art fair.
Dubai Food Festival: February/March — city-wide culinary celebrations.
Dubai World Cup: Late March — world's richest horse race. Spectacular event.
Dubai Summer Surprises: June to September — mall entertainment and deals.
GITEX Technology Week: October — world's largest tech exhibition.
Global Village: October to April — 90-country cultural festival.
Miracle Garden: November to April — world's largest flower garden.
Formula One Abu Dhabi: November (nearby — 1.5 hours from Dubai).
UAE National Day: December 2-3 — spectacular fireworks and celebrations.
Dubai Rugby Sevens: December — iconic sporting and social event.
New Year's Eve Fireworks: December 31 — Burj Khalifa fireworks are world-famous. Book viewing spots weeks in advance.
