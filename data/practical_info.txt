=============================================================
NOVADXB — PRACTICAL INFORMATION GUIDE FOR DUBAI VISITORS
=============================================================

---------------------------------------------------------
VISA & ENTRY
---------------------------------------------------------
Visa on arrival: Citizens of 50+ countries including USA, UK, EU, Australia, Canada receive free 30-day visa on arrival. Extendable once for another 30 days.
GCC residents: Can enter on residency permit — no separate visa needed.
Other nationalities: Must apply for e-visa before travel through Dubai's ICA portal or through Emirates/Flydubai airlines.
Passport validity: Minimum 6 months required from date of entry.
Visa cost: Free for eligible nationalities on arrival. E-visa approximately AED 250-350 for others.
Transit visa: Free 96-hour transit visa available for most nationalities connecting through Dubai Airport.

---------------------------------------------------------
CURRENCY & MONEY
---------------------------------------------------------
Currency: UAE Dirham (AED). 1 USD = approximately 3.67 AED (rate is fixed).
ATMs: Widely available everywhere — airports, malls, metro stations. Use bank ATMs not standalone kiosks for better rates.
Credit cards: Accepted almost universally — Visa and Mastercard everywhere, Amex at larger venues.
Cash: Needed for traditional souks, small cafes, taxis, abra water taxis, and budget local restaurants.
Money exchange: Exchange houses give significantly better rates than airport or bank. Look for Al Ansari Exchange or UAE Exchange branches.
Tipping: Not mandatory but appreciated. 10-15% at restaurants if no service charge added. AED 5-10 for taxi drivers. AED 20-50 for hotel porters per trip.
Budget tip: Most mall ATMs charge no fees for international withdrawals. Airport exchange rates are the worst in the city — change only a small amount at arrival.

---------------------------------------------------------
TRANSPORT
---------------------------------------------------------
Dubai Metro: Clean fast and air-conditioned. Red Line covers most tourist areas — Airport to Downtown to Marina. Green Line covers Deira and Bur Dubai. Single fare AED 3-8.50 depending on zones. Buy a Nol card (AED 25 deposit) for cheaper fares and bus/tram integration.
Nol Card zones: The city is divided into fare zones. From airport to Downtown is approximately AED 5.80 with Nol card.
Taxis: Orange taxis are metered, safe and honest. Starting fare AED 5. Airport taxis AED 25 minimum. Dubai to Marina approximately AED 60-80.
Careem/Uber: Both operate in Dubai. Often slightly cheaper than taxis for shorter trips. App-based so no cash needed.
Dubai Tram: Connects Dubai Marina to JBR area. Integrates with Nol card. Very useful for Marina area.
Abra water taxi: Traditional wooden boats crossing Dubai Creek for AED 1 per person. One of the best authentic experiences in Dubai.
Bus: Extensive network but slower. Useful for areas without metro. Nol card required.
Driving: International licence accepted for tourists. Roads excellent. Parking free at most malls. Salik road toll AED 4 per crossing on some highways.
Important: Drink driving is zero tolerance in UAE. Any alcohol in system while driving is a criminal offence.

---------------------------------------------------------
CLIMATE & BEST TIME TO VISIT
---------------------------------------------------------
October to April: Ideal visiting months. Temperatures 20-32 degrees Celsius. Low humidity. Perfect for outdoor activities beach and desert.
November to March: Peak season. Best weather. Festivals and events. Prices 20-40% higher. Book in advance.
May to September: Extreme summer. 40-48 degrees Celsius with very high humidity in July-August. Outdoor activities impractical in daytime. Everything indoors. Prices significantly lower — best deals.
Ramadan: Dates shift annually. Eating drinking and smoking in public forbidden during daylight. Some restaurants close until sunset. Nightlife limited. Evenings after Iftar are actually a beautiful cultural experience. Respectful dress more important.
UAE National Day: December 2-3 every year. Massive celebrations fireworks and city-wide events. Book accommodation well in advance if visiting.

---------------------------------------------------------
DRESS CODE & CULTURE
---------------------------------------------------------
General rule: Dress modestly in public areas — covered shoulders and knees when not at beach or pool. Swimwear only at beach or pool areas.
Malls and souks: Modest dress required — avoid very short shorts or sleeveless tops. Most malls have cover-up gowns available to borrow at entrance.
Mosques and cultural sites: Women must cover hair arms and legs. Men must cover shoulders and knees. Free abayas available to borrow at most mosques.
Beach: Normal swimwear perfectly acceptable. Topless sunbathing is not permitted anywhere in Dubai.
Nightlife: Smart casual to formal depending on venue. Many rooftop bars and clubs enforce dress codes — no sportswear or flip-flops after 9pm.
Public displays of affection: Keep intimate physical contact private. Kissing in public can attract attention and is technically illegal though rarely enforced for brief moments.
Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people especially Emirati nationals women and in traditional areas. Photographing government buildings military airports is illegal.
Respect: Dubai is an Islamic city. Respect prayer times prayer calls and Islamic practices. Avoid criticism of local rulers religion or government.

---------------------------------------------------------
LANGUAGE
---------------------------------------------------------
Official language: Arabic.
Working language: English — universally spoken in tourism hospitality and business. All signage in English and Arabic.
Other widely spoken: Hindi Urdu Tagalog Malayalam due to large expatriate communities.
Basic Arabic phrases: Marhaba (hello) Shukran (thank you) Afwan (you're welcome) Inshallah (God willing — means maybe) Yalla (let's go/hurry).

---------------------------------------------------------
SAFETY
---------------------------------------------------------
General safety: Dubai is consistently ranked one of the world's safest cities. Violent crime is extremely rare. Petty theft uncommon but not zero in crowded tourist areas.
Emergency numbers: Police 999 / Ambulance 998 / Fire 997 / General emergencies 112.
Medical: World-class hospitals and clinics. Travel insurance strongly recommended as medical costs are high. American Hospital and Mediclinic are most recommended for tourists.
Scams: Very rare in Dubai. Occasional overpricing at tourist-facing restaurants — check menus for prices before ordering.
Legal: Laws are strictly enforced. No drugs zero tolerance. No alcohol in public spaces only licensed venues. No offensive gestures or language in public. VPN use is technically restricted though widely used.
Women travelling solo: Dubai is one of the world's safest cities for solo female travellers. Well-lit transport safe taxis women-only metro carriages available.

---------------------------------------------------------
CONNECTIVITY
---------------------------------------------------------
SIM cards: Buy at airport on arrival. Du or Etisalat (e&). Tourist SIM with data approximately AED 50-100 for 7 days.
Wi-Fi: Free in all malls hotels metro stations and most restaurants. Generally reliable and fast.
VPN: Technically restricted in UAE. VOIP calls (WhatsApp calls FaceTime) are officially blocked though widely accessed via VPN. Use with discretion.
WhatsApp messaging: Fully works. Only the calling function is restricted.
Google Maps: Works perfectly for navigation throughout Dubai.

---------------------------------------------------------
PRAYER TIMES
---------------------------------------------------------
Five daily prayers: Fajr (dawn) Dhuhr (midday) Asr (afternoon) Maghrib (sunset) Isha (night).
During prayer times: Some smaller shops close for 15-20 minutes. Malls and tourist sites remain open. The call to prayer (Adhan) will be heard from mosques — a beautiful and authentic cultural experience.
Friday: The Islamic holy day. Government offices closed. Many businesses open later. Friday brunch is a Dubai institution — the biggest social event of the week.

---------------------------------------------------------
SHOPPING
---------------------------------------------------------
VAT: 5% VAT applies to most goods. Tax-free shopping refund available for tourists at departure airport on purchases over AED 250.
Best malls: Dubai Mall (largest) Mall of the Emirates (ski slope) Ibn Battuta Mall (themed) City Walk (open air premium).
Best souks: Gold Souk (Deira) Spice Souk (Deira) Textile Souk (Bur Dubai) Perfume Souk (Deira).
Bargaining: Expected and acceptable in traditional souks. Not applicable in malls or fixed-price shops. Start at 40-50% of asking price.
Duty free: Dubai Airport duty free is world-famous and genuinely good value — especially for perfume electronics and alcohol.

---------------------------------------------------------
ALCOHOL
---------------------------------------------------------
Availability: Alcohol served in licensed hotels bars and restaurants. Not available in supermarkets — only in licensed liquor stores (MMI and African + Eastern).
Legal drinking age: 21 years.
Public consumption: Illegal to consume alcohol in public spaces streets or parks.
Ramadan: Alcohol service is significantly restricted during Ramadan — many licensed venues temporarily stop serving.
Brunch culture: The Friday brunch with unlimited alcohol is a major Dubai social institution — packages from AED 200-500 per person.
